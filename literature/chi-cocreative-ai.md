# Literature Review: Co-Creative AI and Human-AI Creative Collaboration in HCI

## Key Papers

### 1. Rezwana, J. & Maher, M.L. (2023). Designing Creative AI Partners with COFI: A Framework for Modeling Interaction in Human-AI Co-Creative Systems. *ACM Trans. Computer-Human Interaction*, 30(5), 1–28.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/3519026
arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.07666

- **Contribution:** The COFI (Co-Creative Framework for Interaction design) models participation style, contribution type, and communication in 92 co-creative systems. Identifies gaps in human-AI communication and partnership.
- **Key finding:** Most co-creative systems lack genuine bidirectional communication; AI is typically reactive, not proactive.
- **Relevance:** Our improvisation themes (active listening, empathy, trust) directly address COFI's identified gap in human-AI communication. Improvisation theory explains what genuine creative partnership requires.

### 2. Rezwana, J. & Maher, M.L. (2026). Improving User Experience with FAICO: Towards a Framework for AI Communication in Human-AI Co-Creativity. In *Proc. CHI EA 2026*. ACM.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/3706599.3719858

- **Contribution:** Extension of COFI focusing on AI communication modalities in co-creative systems.
- **Relevance:** Continued evolution of co-creative frameworks; our improvisation-informed framework is complementary.

### 3. CreAItive Collaboration? Users' Misjudgment of AI-Creativity Affects Their Collaborative Performance. (2025). In *Proc. CHI 2025*. ACM.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/3706598.3713886

- **Contribution:** Studies how users' perception of AI creativity affects collaborative outcomes. Users who overestimate AI creativity perform differently.
- **Relevance:** Connects to our "non-judgmental flow" and "empathy" themes — how humans perceive AI creative agency matters for improvisation quality.

### 4. Adobe Research (2025). Compositional Structures as Substrates for Human-AI Co-creation Environment. In *Proc. CHI 2025*. ACM.
URL: https://research.adobe.com/news/an-experimental-new-design-approach-for-human-ai-co-creation/

- **Contribution:** New design approach for environments supporting iterative human-AI co-creation across entire creative processes.
- **Relevance:** System-level approach to co-creativity; our framework provides theoretical grounding for why certain substrates support creative emergence.

### 5. Kantosalo, A. & Toivonen, H. (2016). Modes of Creative Human-Computer Collaboration. In *Proc. ICCC 2016*.
URL: https://computationalcreativity.net/iccc2016/wp-content/uploads/2016/01/Modes-of-Creative-Human-Computer-Collaboration.pdf

- **Contribution:** Identifies alternating and parallel modes of human-AI creative collaboration.
- **Relevance:** These modes map onto improvisational interaction patterns (call-and-response, simultaneous play, etc.).

### 6. Yannakakis, G., Liapis, A., & Alexopoulos, C. (2014). Mixed-Initiative Co-Creativity. In *Proc. FDG 2014*.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.17083/ijsg.v1i1.15

- **Contribution:** Framework for mixed-initiative systems in game design where both human and AI contribute creatively.
- **Relevance:** Mixed-initiative as structured improvisation — our framework explains the dynamics underlying successful mixed-initiative interaction.

### 7. Soros, L.B., Guckelsberger, C., & Stanley, K. (2024). On Creativity and Open-Endedness. In *Proc. ALIFE 2024*.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1162/isal_a_00801

- **Contribution:** Bridges computational creativity and open-endedness. CC evaluates products; OE studies processes. Identifies the process/product tension.
- **Relevance:** We add improvisation as the third pillar — where process IS product. Our CHI paper makes this triangulation accessible to HCI audience.

### 8. Deterding, S. (2023). The Design of AI-Powered Creative Tools: A Critical Review of Co-Creative AI. *Human-Computer Interaction*.
URL: https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/07370024.2023.2227tried

- **Contribution:** Critical review of co-creative AI design, identifying patterns and gaps in how creative AI tools are designed.
- **Relevance:** Design patterns for co-creative AI; our improvisation principles can inform these patterns.

### 9. Muller, M., et al. (2024). Human-AI Co-Creativity: Exploring Synergies Across Levels of Creative Collaboration. arXiv:2411.12527.
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.12527

- **Contribution:** Explores multiple levels of creative collaboration between humans and AI, from tool-use to genuine partnership.
- **Relevance:** Our improvisation framework specifically addresses the highest level — genuine creative partnership — which requires the relational qualities our themes identify.

### 10. Frontiers (2025). Exploring Creativity in Human-AI Co-Creation: A Comparative Study Across Design Experience.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.3389/fcomp.2025.1672735

- **Contribution:** Compares how designers with different experience levels engage in co-creative AI processes.
- **Relevance:** Experience/expertise affects co-creative dynamics — parallels our expert improviser interviews showing practiced skills matter.

## Key Gaps in Co-Creative AI Literature

1. **No open-endedness framework** — Co-creative systems aim for sustained creativity but don't use OE theory to understand or evaluate it
2. **Missing improvisation theory** — Systems borrow superficially from improv (e.g., "yes, and...") without deep engagement with improvisation scholarship
3. **Embodiment under-addressed** — Most co-creative AI work is screen-based; embodied co-creative improvisation (LuminAI excepted) is rare
4. **Relational qualities absent** — Trust, vulnerability, care (central to improvisation) are not modeled in co-creative frameworks like COFI
5. **No temporal/processual evaluation** — Co-creative systems are evaluated on output quality, not on whether they sustain generative creative processes over time (an OE concern)

## Positioning Opportunity

Our paper can position itself at the intersection of:
- **COFI** (interaction framework) — we add the relational/improvisational dimension COFI lacks
- **LuminAI** (embodied co-creative system) — we provide the OE-informed theoretical framework for understanding why it works
- **Soros et al.** (CC-OE bridge) — we bring this to HCI with improvisation as the mediating concept
- **GravField** (Amber's own system) — we have a working design exemplar
