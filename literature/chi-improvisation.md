# Literature Review: Improvisation in HCI/Interaction Design

## Key Papers

### 1. Trajkova, M., Long, D., Deshpande, M., Knowlton, A., & Magerko, B. (2024). Exploring Collaborative Movement Improvisation Towards the Design of LuminAI — a Co-Creative AI Dance Partner. In *Proc. CHI 2024*. ACM.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/3613904.3642677

- **Contribution:** Focus groups with 24 university dance students on embodied dyadic improvisation. Develops an "Interconnected Model of Improvisational Dance Inputs" and design recommendations for LuminAI.
- **Key findings:** Dancers value responsiveness, surprise, and the sense of genuine partnership. Improvisation requires continuous mutual adaptation.
- **Relevance:** Most directly comparable CHI paper on dance improvisation + AI. Our work goes beyond dance to multiple improvisation traditions and connects to open-endedness theory, which LuminAI work does not.

### 2. Trajkova, M., et al. (2025). Bringing LuminAI to Life: Studying Dancers' Perceptions of a Co-Creative AI in Dance Improvisation Class. In *Proc. C&C 2025*. ACM.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/3698061.3726925

- **Contribution:** Seven-month, four-phase collaboration with 15 dancers. Contextual diary study and culminating performance. Deepest longitudinal study of human-AI dance improvisation to date.
- **Key findings:** AI as catalyst, constraint, and co-creator in dance. Dancers develop evolving relationships with AI partner over time.
- **Relevance:** Demonstrates the value of extended engagement studies. Our work provides the theoretical framework that explains WHY these dynamics emerge (through OE lens).

### 3. Trajkova, M., et al. (2025). LuminAI: Embodied AI as a Catalyst, Constraint, and Co-Creator in Dance. In *Proc. ICCC 2025*.
URL: https://computationalcreativity.net/iccc25/wp-content/uploads/papers/iccc25-trajkova2025luminai.pdf

- **Contribution:** Synthesizes LuminAI research program, positioning embodied AI as playing three roles in creative dance.
- **Relevance:** The catalyst/constraint/co-creator taxonomy maps onto our improvisation themes.

### 4. Hu, B., Tao, Y., Lin, R., Chai, M., Huang, Y., & Patibanda, R. (2026). Nudging the Somas: Exploring How Live-Configurable Mixed Reality Objects Shape Open-Ended Intercorporeal Movements. In *Proc. CHI 2026*. ACM.
arXiv: https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.14432

- **Contribution:** GravField system — live-configurable MR objects with "digital physics" shape emergent intercorporeal movement. Research-through-design study showing ambiguity + real-time configuration sustain open-ended exploration.
- **Relevance:** THIS IS AMBER'S OWN CHI 2026 PAPER. Directly demonstrates open-ended improvisation in MR. The CHI 2027 paper can reference this as a design exemplar that embodies the theoretical principles.

### 5. AI Meets Holographic Pepper's Ghost: A Co-Creative Public Dance Experience. (2023). In *Proc. DIS 2023 Companion*. ACM.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/3563703.3596658

- **Contribution:** Public installation combining AI-generated dance with holographic display. Explores audience engagement with AI dance improvisation.
- **Relevance:** Shows interest in public-facing AI improvisation systems in HCI community.

### 6. Cetin, M., et al. (2025). AI See, You See: Human-AI Musical Collaboration in Augmented Reality. In *Proc. CHI EA 2025*. ACM.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/3706599.3720052

- **Contribution:** Tests co-creative musical improvisation with two humans and an AI agent in AR. Investigates how AR interfaces affect perception of AI's role.
- **Relevance:** Recent CHI work on musical improvisation + AI, showing the topic's currency in HCI.

### 7. Spence, J. (2025). Artificial Intelligence in Dance Choreography: A Practice-as-Research Exploration of Human-AI Co-Creation Using ChatGPT-4. *Intl. J. Performance Arts and Digital Media*, 21(2).
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1080/14794713.2025.2515754

- **Contribution:** Practice-as-research on LLM-based dance co-creation. How text-based AI translates to embodied improvisational practice.
- **Relevance:** Emerging LLM-improvisation intersection; our framework can explain why text-to-embodiment translation is generative.

### 8. Huang, Y., et al. (2025). Revival: Collaborative Artistic Creation Through Human-AI Interactions in Musical Creativity. arXiv:2503.15498.
URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.15498

- **Contribution:** Real-time co-creative improvisation between human performers and AI agents trained on deceased composers' works.
- **Relevance:** State-of-the-art in human-AI musical improvisation; demonstrates the convergence of human improv practice and AI capabilities.

### 9. Ladenheim, K., et al. (2024). Human-AI Co-Dancing: Evolving Cultural Heritage Through Collaborative Choreography with Generative Virtual Characters. In *Proc. MOCO 2024*. ACM.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/3658852.3661317

- **Contribution:** AI dance partners powered by algorithms from traditional Thai dance. Real-time movement generation in cultural heritage context.
- **Relevance:** Cross-cultural improvisation + AI — relevant to our call for non-Western perspectives.

### 10. Suchman, L. (2007). *Human-Machine Reconfigurations: Plans and Situated Actions.* Cambridge University Press.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511808418

- **Contribution:** Foundational HCI text arguing all action is improvised/situated; plans are resources, not scripts.
- **Relevance:** Theoretical anchor for understanding improvisation as fundamental to all interaction, not just artistic performance.

## Emerging Themes in CHI Improvisation Research

1. **Embodiment is central** — Most impactful work involves bodily, physical interaction (dance, movement, haptics), not just screen-based
2. **Longitudinal engagement** — Moving from one-shot studies to extended collaborations (LuminAI's 7-month study)
3. **Cultural sensitivity** — Growing attention to non-Western traditions and power dynamics
4. **AR/MR as improvisation medium** — Amber's GravField and Cetin's AR music show MR as emerging medium for improvisation
5. **LLMs changing the landscape** — Text-based AI entering embodied improvisation (Spence 2025)
6. **Open-endedness as under-theorized** — Systems aim for open-ended behavior but lack formal OE framework (our gap)
