# Literature Review: Open-Endedness in HCI/Interactive Systems

## Key Papers

### 1. Boon, B. & Rozendaal, M. (2019). Ambiguity and Open-Endedness in Behavioural Design. In *Proc. DIS 2019*. ACM.
URL: https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Ambiguity-and-Open-Endedness-in-Behavioural-Design-Boon-Rozendaal/36696c5e843d42a80eccf2ae9b5f6199fa898bb7

- **Contribution:** Examines how ambiguity and open-endedness in design afford multiple interpretations and courses of action. Connects to Gaver et al.'s work on ambiguity as a design resource.
- **Relevance:** Directly addresses open-endedness as a design property in HCI. Our work deepens this with formal OE theory from ALife.

### 2. Gaver, W., Beaver, J., & Benford, S. (2003). Ambiguity as a Resource for Design. In *Proc. CHI 2003*. ACM.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/642611.642653

- **Contribution:** Seminal CHI paper arguing ambiguity is a positive design resource, enabling multiple interpretations and open-ended engagement.
- **Relevance:** Foundational for understanding why open-endedness matters in HCI. Our work extends this with formal OE mechanisms.

### 3. Sengers, P. & Gaver, W. (2006). Staying Open to Interpretation: Engaging Multiple Meanings in Design and Evaluation. In *Proc. DIS 2006*. ACM.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/1142405.1142422

- **Contribution:** Argues against closing down interpretation in interactive system design; evaluation should embrace multiple meanings.
- **Relevance:** Philosophical alignment with our argument that open-endedness (sustained novelty, multiple outcomes) is valuable in interactive systems.

### 4. Velt, R., Benford, S., & Reeves, S. (2017). A Survey of the Trajectories Conceptual Framework. In *Proc. DIS 2017*. ACM.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/3064663.3064666

- **Contribution:** Maps how users move through interactive experiences over time. Trajectories as open-ended paths through designed experiences.
- **Relevance:** Temporal dimension of open-ended interaction; connects to our "being in the time" theme.

### 5. Giaccardi, E. & Redström, J. (2020). Technology and More-Than-Human Design. *Design Issues*, 36(4), 33–44.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1162/desi_a_00612

- **Contribution:** When non-human entities (AI, materials, environments) have agency, design becomes inherently open-ended. Design shifts from controlling outcomes to enabling emergence.
- **Relevance:** Core theoretical connection — more-than-human design implies open-endedness. Our work formalizes this link.

### 6. Benford, S. et al. (2020). Technology for Situated and Emergent Play: A Bridging Concept and Design Agenda. In *Proc. CHI 2020*. ACM.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/3313831.3376859

- **Contribution:** Designs for play that emerges from situated contexts rather than pre-designed game mechanics. Embraces unpredictability and open-ended exploration.
- **Relevance:** Emergent play is improvisation by another name. Connects our themes (playfulness, structure/surprise balance) to HCI play research.

### 7. Höök, K. (2018). *Designing with the Body: Somaesthetic Interaction Design.* MIT Press.
DOI: https://doi.org/10.7551/mitpress/11481.001.0001

- **Contribution:** First book on somaesthetic design — using bodily, felt experience as the basis for interaction design. Improvisation is central to soma design practice.
- **Relevance:** Direct connection to our "active listening" and "being in the time" themes. Soma design IS improvisational design.

### 8. Hu, B. et al. (2026). Nudging the Somas (CHI 2026). [See chi-improvisation.md #4]
- **Relevance to OE:** Demonstrates open-ended intercorporeal movement as design outcome. Amber's own work proving OE concepts work in MR interaction.

### 9. Ryan, J., Mateas, M., & Wardrip-Fruin, N. (2015). Open Design Challenges for Interactive Emergent Narrative. In *Interactive Storytelling (ICIDS 2015)*. Springer.
URL: https://eis.ucsc.edu/papers/ryanEtAl_OpenDesignChallengesForInteractiveEmergentNarrative.pdf

- **Contribution:** Identifies design challenges for stories that emerge bottom-up from autonomous character behavior. Open-ended narrative as a design goal.
- **Relevance:** Narrative open-endedness parallels our improvisational open-endedness. Similar design challenges (balancing structure/surprise).

### 10. Deterding, S., et al. (2017). Mixed-Initiative Creative Interfaces. In *Proc. CHI 2017 Workshop*. ACM.

- **Contribution:** Framework for systems where both human and AI take creative initiative, negotiating control in real-time.
- **Relevance:** Mixed-initiative as a form of improvisation. Our framework explains WHY mixed-initiative works (through OE dynamics).

## Open-Endedness in HCI: The Gap

The HCI community has long valued open-endedness in design (ambiguity, appropriation, emergence, playfulness) but has NOT connected this to the formal OE research from ALife/evolutionary computation. Key gaps:

1. **No formal OE metrics in HCI** — HCI evaluates engagement, usability, experience; not sustained novelty generation or complexity growth
2. **No OE-informed design framework** — Designers create for open-endedness intuitively; no formal principles from OE theory
3. **Improvisation as bridge** — Both HCI and ALife study open-endedness but in different vocabularies; improvisation theory can mediate
4. **Amber's unique position** — Author of both a CHI 2026 paper on open-ended MR interaction AND the WIP connecting improvisation to OE
