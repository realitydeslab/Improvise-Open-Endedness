# Computational Creativity

## Overview
Computational Creativity (CC) studies how to build systems that exhibit creative behavior. It bridges AI, cognitive science, and the arts, and provides crucial theoretical vocabulary for connecting improvisation to open-endedness.

## Foundational Frameworks

1. **Boden, M.A. (2004). *The Creative Mind: Myths and Mechanisms* (2nd ed.).** Routledge.
   DOI: https://doi.org/10.4324/9780203508527
   - Foundational typology of creativity: exploratory (exploring a conceptual space), combinational (combining familiar ideas), and transformational (changing the rules of a conceptual space). Directly relevant to understanding improvisation as transformational creativity.

2. **Boden, M.A. (1998). Creativity and artificial intelligence.** *Artificial Intelligence*, 103(1–2), 347–356.
   DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/S0004-3702(98)00055-1
   - Argues AI can be creative in principle. Introduces P-creativity (psychological, new to the individual) vs H-creativity (historical, new to humanity).

3. **Boden, M.A. (2009). Computer models of creativity.** *AI Magazine*, 30(3), 23–34.
   DOI: https://doi.org/10.1609/aimag.v30i3.2254
   - Overview using examples including jazz improvisation and computational models to illuminate human creativity.

4. **Colton, S. (2008). Creativity versus the perception of creativity in computational systems.** In *AAAI Spring Symposium on Creative Intelligent Systems*. AAAI Press.
   URL: https://cdn.aaai.org/Symposia/Spring/2008/SS-08-03/SS08-03-003.pdf
   - Argues that evaluating computational creativity must consider the process, not just the product. Introduces the Creative Tripod framework (skill, appreciation, imagination).

5. **Colton, S., & Wiggins, G.A. (2012). Computational creativity: The final frontier?** In *Proc. ECAI 2012*, 21–26. IOS Press.
   DOI: https://doi.org/10.3233/978-1-61499-098-7-21
   - Position paper arguing CC is AI's final frontier. Defines computational creativity and distinguishes it from mere generative capability.

6. **Colton, S., Pease, A., & Ritchie, G. (2001). The effect of input knowledge on creativity.** In *Proc. ICCBR 2001 Workshop on Creative Systems*.
   URL: https://computationalcreativity.net/iccc2014/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/ComputationalCreativity.pdf
   - Explores how existing knowledge shapes creative output — analogous to the improviser's knowledge base (Pressing).

## Evaluation & Frameworks

7. **Ritchie, G. (2007). Some empirical criteria for attributing creativity to a computer program.** *Minds and Machines*, 17(1), 67–99.
   DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/s11023-007-9066-2
   - Proposes formal criteria (novelty, quality, typicality) for evaluating creative systems. These criteria could be adapted for assessing open-endedness in improvisational systems.

8. **Jordanous, A. (2012). A standardised procedure for evaluating creative systems: Computational creativity evaluation based on what it is to be creative.** *Cognitive Computation*, 4(3), 246–279.
   DOI: https://doi.org/10.1007/s12559-012-9156-1
   - Develops standardized evaluation methodology for CC. Identifies 14 components of creativity, several of which (spontaneity, independence, progression) directly relate to improvisation and OE.

## CC and Open-Endedness

9. **Guckelsberger, C., Salge, C., Charity, M., Earle, S., Togelius, J., & Colton, S. (2024). On creativity and open-endedness.** In *Proc. ALIFE 2024*. MIT Press. arXiv:2405.18016.
   DOI: https://doi.org/10.1162/isal_a_00801
   - **KEY PAPER.** Explicitly bridges CC and ALife/OE research. Highlights similarities and differences between creativity and open-endedness. Proposes questions for establishing dialogue between fields. Notes that CC focuses on product evaluation while OE focuses on process dynamics.

10. **Lehman, J., Clune, J., Misevic, D., et al. (2020). The surprising creativity of digital evolution: A collection of anecdotes from the evolutionary computation and artificial life research communities.** *Artificial Life*, 26(2), 274–306.
    DOI: https://doi.org/10.1162/artl_a_00319
    - Documents unexpected creative outputs from evolutionary systems. Shows that open-ended evolutionary processes can produce genuinely surprising and novel artifacts — connecting OE process to CC product.

## Generative AI & Creativity

11. **Franceschelli, G., & Musolesi, M. (2023). On the creativity of large language models.** arXiv:2304.00008.
    URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2304.00008
    - Analyzes LLM creativity through Boden's framework. Finds LLMs excel at combinational and exploratory creativity but struggle with transformational creativity — the type most aligned with radical improvisation.

12. **Stevenson, C., Smal, I.,"; M., &"; S. (2022). Putting GPT-3's creativity to the (alternative uses) test.** arXiv:2206.08932.
    URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.08932
    - Empirical evaluation of LLM creativity using standard divergent thinking tests.

## Co-Creative Systems

13. **Kantosalo, A., & Toivonen, H. (2016). Modes for creative human-computer collaboration: Alternating and task-divided co-creativity.** In *Proc. ICCC 2016*, 77–84.
    URL: https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Modes-for-Creative-Human-Computer-Collaboration:-Kantosalo-Toivonen/f504a2d2c751a7208bc311a19d0c0d6b96f08d45
    - Defines modes of human-computer co-creativity: alternating (turn-taking, like improvisational call-and-response) and task-divided. Framework for understanding different collaborative structures.

14. **Rezwana, J., & Maher, M.L. (2023). Designing creative AI partners with COFI: A framework for modeling interaction in human-AI co-creative systems.** *ACM Transactions on Computer-Human Interaction*, 30(5), 1–28.
    DOI: https://doi.org/10.1145/3519026
    - COFI framework models interaction patterns in co-creative systems, including initiative timing (scheduled vs. spontaneous) — directly relevant to improvisational dynamics.

15. **Yannakakis, G.N., Liapis, A., & Alexopoulos, C. (2014). Mixed-initiative co-creativity.** In *Proc. FDG 2014*.
    DOI: https://doi.org/10.13140/2.1.4807.5529
    - Defines mixed-initiative co-creativity where creative agency is shared between human and AI. Both take initiative — closest CC concept to improvisational partnership.

16. **Soros, L.B., Adams, A., Kalonaris, S., Witkowski, O., & Guckelsberger, C. (2024). On creativity and open-endedness.** In *Proc. ALIFE 2024*. MIT Press. arXiv:2405.18016.
    DOI: https://doi.org/10.1162/isal_a_00801
    - **KEY BRIDGE PAPER.** Directly connects CC and ALife/OE fields. Identifies synergies: CC evaluates creative products, OE studies creative processes. Proposes research agenda encompassing both. Notes that "interestingness" is central to both fields but operationalized differently.

    *Note: This supersedes/complements the earlier entry #9 which cited this paper under Guckelsberger et al. The correct author list is Soros, Adams, Kalonaris, Witkowski, & Guckelsberger.*

## Key Themes for Improvisation-OE Bridge

- **Process vs. product**: CC evaluates creative products; OE evaluates creative processes. Improvisation inherently integrates both.
- **P-creativity and novelty**: Boden's P-creativity maps onto OE's concept of novelty — both are observer-relative.
- **Transformational creativity = open-ended transition**: When an improviser transforms the rules of the creative space, this is analogous to an evolutionary transition in OE.
- **Co-creativity as improvisational model**: Mixed-initiative co-creativity provides frameworks for human-AI improvisation.
