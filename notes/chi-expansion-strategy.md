#### 3. Formal Conceptual Framework (KEY CONTRIBUTION)
Move from a list of 8 themes to a structured framework:

**Proposed Framework: "Improvisational Open-Endedness" (IOE)**

Three layers:
1. **Attentional Layer** — Active listening, being in the time, embracing the unknown
   → Maps to: environmental sensing, real-time responsiveness, stochastic exploration
2. **Relational Layer** — Empathy, trust, boundaries, care; non-judgmental flow
   → Maps to: mutual modeling, constraint respect, acceptance dynamics
3. **Generative Layer** — Balancing structure/surprise, imaginative metaphor, playfulness
   → Maps to: edge-of-chaos dynamics, cross-modal transfer, intrinsic motivation

Each layer has:
- Improvisation concept (from interviews)
- OE mechanism (from ALife literature)
- Design principle (for HCI practitioners)
- Design exemplar (from existing systems including GravField)

**Visual:** Circular/layered diagram showing the three layers with improvisation concepts on one side and OE mechanisms on the other, connected by design principles.

#### 4. Design Implications Section (REQUIRED for CHI)
For each theme/principle, provide:
- **What to design:** Concrete system features or interaction patterns
- **How to evaluate:** Metrics inspired by both improvisation and OE
- **Example:** Reference to GravField or LuminAI as embodiment

Example design implications:
- **DI1: Design for Active Listening** — Systems should sense and respond to user behavior at multiple timescales (micro-gestures, movement phrases, session-level patterns), not just aggregate input
- **DI2: Design for Temporal Immediacy** — Interaction should emphasize present-moment experience, not goal-directed planning
- **DI3: Design for Productive Uncertainty** — Introduce controlled stochasticity that users can learn to work with
- **DI4: Design for Non-Judgmental Exploration** — Remove or defer evaluation during creative phases; separate exploration from assessment
- **DI5: Design for Structure-Surprise Balance** — Provide adjustable constraints that maintain the system at the "edge of chaos"
- **DI6: Design for Cross-Modal Transfer** — Enable metaphorical mappings between sensory modalities
- **DI7: Design for Relational Trust** — System behavior should be consistent enough to trust but surprising enough to inspire
- **DI8: Design for Intrinsic Motivation** — Reward exploration and play, not just task completion
