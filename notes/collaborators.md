# Collaborator Discovery: Improvisation and Open-Endedness

## Recommended Collaborators

### 1. Oliver Bown
- **Affiliation:** UNSW Sydney, Interactive Media Lab
- **Key Papers:** Bown et al. (2025) "On Improvisation and Open-Endedness"; Bown (2021) *Beyond the Creative Species*
- **Collab Angle:** Authors of the most directly relevant paper. Natural collaborators for developing the improvisation-OE bridge further. Bown brings deep expertise in computational creativity and ALife art. Could co-develop the theoretical framework or run joint empirical studies.
- **Priority:** ★★★ Highest

### 2. Petra Gemeinboeck
- **Affiliation:** UNSW Sydney, Art & Design
- **Key Papers:** Co-author on Bown et al. (2025); known for robotic art and performative installations
- **Collab Angle:** Expert in embodied/performative AI. Could contribute the artistic practice-based research dimension. Connects robotic embodiment with improvisational practice.
- **Priority:** ★★★ High

### 3. Lisa B. Soros
- **Affiliation:** Cross Labs, Cross Compass
- **Key Papers:** Soros et al. (2024) "On Creativity and Open-Endedness"; Soros & Stanley (2014) Chromaria
- **Collab Angle:** Lead author on the CC-OE bridge paper. Adding improvisation as a third vertex to her CC-OE framework is a natural extension. Could co-write the formalized bridge paper.
- **Priority:** ★★★ High

### 4. Keith Sawyer
- **Affiliation:** University of North Carolina, School of Education
- **Key Papers:** Sawyer (2003) *Group Creativity*; Sawyer (2006) on collaborative emergence
- **Collab Angle:** Leading theorist of collaborative emergence in improvisation. His frameworks are foundational for connecting group improvisation to multi-agent OE. Could validate/extend the theoretical mapping.
- **Priority:** ★★ High

### 5. David Borgo
- **Affiliation:** UC San Diego, Music Department
- **Key Papers:** Borgo (2005) *Sync or Swarm*; complexity theory analysis of free improvisation
- **Collab Angle:** Pioneered the complexity-theory framing of improvisation that maps directly onto OE dynamics. Could help formalize the edge-of-chaos connection between improvisation and OE.
- **Priority:** ★★ High

### 6. Kristina Höök
- **Affiliation:** KTH Royal Institute of Technology, Stockholm
- **Key Papers:** Höök (2018) *Designing with the Body*; soma design research program
- **Collab Angle:** Leading somaesthetic design researcher. Her work on embodied improvisation in design could inform "embodied open-endedness." Could co-design somatic workshops exploring OE through movement improvisation.
- **Priority:** ★★ Medium-High

### 7. Milka Trajkova
- **Affiliation:** Georgia Institute of Technology
- **Key Papers:** Trajkova et al. (2024, 2025) LuminAI papers at CHI and C&C
- **Collab Angle:** Lead researcher on LuminAI — the most developed embodied human-AI dance improvisation system. Could collaborate on empirical studies of open-endedness in human-AI dance improvisation using LuminAI.
- **Priority:** ★★ Medium-High

### 8. Jeff Clune
- **Affiliation:** University of British Columbia / Vector Institute
- **Key Papers:** OMNI/OMNI-EPIC; "Open-endedness is essential for ASI"; MAP-Elites
- **Collab Angle:** Leading OE researcher. His work on interestingness-driven OE connects directly to improvisational aesthetic judgment. Could co-develop "improvisation-informed interestingness" measures for OE systems.
- **Priority:** ★★ Medium-High

### 9. George Lewis
- **Affiliation:** Columbia University, Music Department
- **Key Papers:** Lewis (2000) Voyager; Lewis (1996) Afrological/Eurological perspectives
- **Collab Angle:** Pioneer of human-computer improvisation. His work on machine subjectivity in improvisation connects to agent ethology. Could provide the music theory depth and cultural analysis dimension.
- **Priority:** ★★ Medium

### 10. Christian Guckelsberger
- **Affiliation:** Aalto University / IT University of Copenhagen
- **Key Papers:** Co-author on Soros et al. (2024); embodiment in computational creativity
- **Collab Angle:** Works at the CC-OE intersection with focus on embodiment. Could bridge the embodied improvisation and computational creativity dimensions.
- **Priority:** ★★ Medium

### 11. Jon McCormack
- **Affiliation:** Monash University, SensiLab
- **Key Papers:** McCormack (2001) Eden; McCormack (2005) open problems in evolutionary music/art
- **Collab Angle:** Long track record in evolutionary art and ALife creativity. His systems (Eden) are designed for open-endedness. Could bring the generative/evolutionary art practice perspective.
- **Priority:** ★ Medium

### 12. Joel Lehman
- **Affiliation:** Anthropic (previously Uber AI Labs)
- **Key Papers:** Lehman & Stanley (2011) novelty search; Lehman et al. (2020) surprising creativity
- **Collab Angle:** Co-inventor of novelty search and collaborator of Amber's at ERA. Could help formalize the connection between objective-free search and improvisational practice. Already an advisor — natural overlap.
- **Priority:** ★★★ Already connected
