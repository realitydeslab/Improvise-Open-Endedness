## The Specific Gap in CHI Literature

**No existing CHI paper connects improvisation theory to open-endedness formalism to produce design implications for co-creative/interactive systems.**

- LuminAI studies improvisation empirically but doesn't engage with OE theory
- COFI models interaction but misses relational/improvisational qualities
- Ambiguity/emergence literature values open-endedness but lacks formal OE framework
- Soros et al. bridge CC-OE but don't address HCI/design implications
- Soma design uses improvisation as practice but doesn't formalize it

**Our unique contribution:** We triangulate improvisation theory, OE formalism, and HCI design to produce:
1. A formal framework mapping improv concepts → OE mechanisms → design principles
2. Actionable design implications for co-creative and open-ended interactive systems
3. Grounded in expert interviews AND demonstrated through our own system (GravField)

## Positioning Strategy for CHI

**Frame as:** "From Improvisation to Open-Ended Interaction: A Framework for Designing Co-Creative Systems"

**Narrative arc:**
1. Co-creative AI systems aspire to open-ended creative partnership, but lack theoretical grounding
2. Improvisation theory offers deep understanding of how creative novelty is sustained
3. Open-endedness research from ALife provides formal mechanisms for sustained novelty
4. We bridge all three through expert interviews + formal framework + design implications
5. Validated through our own system design (GravField, CHI 2026)
