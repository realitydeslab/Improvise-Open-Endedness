## Detailed Positioning

### 1. Hu (2025) — OUR WIP Paper Being Expanded

**arXiv: 2511.00529**

This is our own work-in-progress paper (sole author: Botao 'Amber' Hu), submitted to AAAI 2026 CLIP workshop. It interviews 6 improvisation experts and identifies 8 key themes connecting improvisation to experiential AI design.

**What the CHI expansion adds:**
- Move beyond **experiential AI** framing to **open-endedness** as a formal property of creative systems
- Build a **formal conceptual framework** with explicit mappings between improvisational concepts and OE mechanisms
- Develop **design implications** for building open-ended interactive systems informed by improvisation
- Add **deeper thematic analysis** and potentially more participants
- Include **CHI-required sections**: design implications, broader impact, formal evaluation

### 2. Soros et al. (2024) — Key Bridge Paper

**DOI: 10.1162/isal_a_00801**

Bridges CC and ALife/OE research. Identifies that CC evaluates creative products while OE studies creative processes.

**Our differentiation:**
- They bridge CC ↔ OE. We add **improvisation** as the third vertex of a triangle: CC (product) — OE (process) — Improvisation (process-as-product).
- Improvisation uniquely integrates both process and product evaluation — the performance IS the creation. This resolves a tension Soros et al. identify but don't resolve.

### 3. Borgo (2005) — Complexity Theory Connection

Maps free improvisation onto complexity theory (edge of chaos, self-organization, emergence).

**Our differentiation:**
- Borgo uses complexity theory as an interpretive lens for improvisation. We use it as a **formal bridge** to OE, connecting Borgo's musical insights to Langton's edge-of-chaos and Taylor et al.'s OE requirements.
- We extend to multi-agent AI systems, not just human musicians.

### 4. Stanley & Lehman (2015) — Objective-Free Philosophy

**Our differentiation:**
- They argue philosophically for objective-free search. We show how improvisers **actually practice** objective-free creative search in real-time, providing concrete examples of the strategies, heuristics, and social negotiations involved.
- We translate improvisational strategies ("yes, and...", constraint-based play, responsive listening) into formal OE design patterns.

## Summary of Novel Contributions

Our project uniquely:
1. **Triangulates** CC, OE, and improvisation theory (no existing work does this)
2. **Formalizes** the improvisation–OE connection (our WIP identified themes; the full paper formalizes them)
3. Proposes **design principles** for open-ended systems from improvisation practice
4. Develops **embodied open-endedness** as a concept (bridging Höök and ALife/OE)
5. Connects **agent ethology** to improvisational behavior in multi-agent systems
