# Research Plan: Improvisation and Open-Endedness

## Research Questions

### RQ1: What are the structural correspondences between improvisational practice and open-ended processes?
- Map concepts from improvisation theory (collaborative emergence, constraint-based creativity, edge of chaos, "yes and...") to formal concepts in OE research (novelty search, quality-diversity, major transitions, autocurricula)
- Identify where the analogy holds rigorously and where it breaks down
- Develop a formal conceptual framework: the Improvisation-Open-Endedness (IOE) mapping

### RQ2: Can improvisational practices yield design principles for building open-ended systems?
- Translate improvisational strategies (constraint navigation, responsive listening, risk-taking, "not knowing") into algorithmic or architectural design patterns for OE systems
- Test: do OE systems designed with improvisational principles produce more sustained novelty than baseline systems?

### RQ3: How does open-endedness manifest in human-AI improvisational systems?
- Define and operationalize "open-endedness" for interactive creative systems (not just evolutionary systems)
- Develop metrics that capture improvisational qualities: responsiveness, risk, coherence, collaborative emergence
- Apply these metrics to existing human-AI improvisation systems (LuminAI, Voyager, Shimon)

### RQ4: What role does embodiment play in open-ended creative processes?
- Investigate whether embodied (somaesthetic, contact improvisation) interaction produces different OE dynamics than disembodied interaction
- Design and evaluate an embodied human-AI improvisation system through the IOE framework

### RQ5 (stretch): Can we observe improvisational behavior emerging in multi-agent AI systems?
- Using agent ethology methods, observe emergent behavior in multi-agent systems that resembles improvisation
- Characterize the conditions under which agent "improvisation" emerges
- Connect to Amber's agent ethology research program

## Methodology

### Phase 1: Theoretical Framework (Months 1–4)
**Method:** Conceptual analysis and framework development
- Systematic mapping of improvisation concepts to OE concepts
- Develop the IOE (Improvisation-Open-Endedness) conceptual framework
- Formalize design principles derived from improvisation for OE systems
- **Output:** Framework paper (target: ALIFE or Artificial Life journal)

### Phase 2: Metrics & Evaluation (Months 3–6)
**Method:** Mixed methods — formal metric development + expert evaluation
- Develop "improvisational open-endedness" metrics combining OE measures (novelty, diversity, complexity growth) with improvisation qualities (responsiveness, risk, emergence)
- Validate metrics with improvisation experts and OE researchers
- Apply to existing systems retrospectively
- **Output:** Metrics paper (target: CHI or C&C)

### Phase 3: Empirical Studies (Months 5–10)
**Method:** Practice-based research + computational experiments

**Study A: Human-AI Improvisation Analysis**
- Analyze existing human-AI improvisation sessions using IOE framework
- Collaborative study with LuminAI team (Trajkova) or similar system
- Mixed methods: video analysis, movement data, participant interviews, IOE metrics

**Study B: Agent-Based Improvisation Simulation**
- Design multi-agent systems with improvisation-informed interaction rules
- Compare OE dynamics of improvisation-inspired agents vs. standard evolutionary agents
- Measure: novelty generation rate, diversity maintenance, complexity growth, emergence
- **Output:** Empirical paper (target: ALIFE, CHI, or CSCW)

### Phase 4: Design & Reflection (Months 8–12)
**Method:** Research through design + reflection
- Design an embodied human-AI improvisation system informed by the IOE framework
- Deploy in workshop/performance settings
- Evaluate open-endedness of interactions using developed metrics
- **Output:** Design paper (target: CHI, DIS, or TEI)

## Contributions

1. **The IOE Framework:** A formal conceptual framework mapping improvisation theory onto OE research, identifying structural correspondences, tensions, and productive gaps. This is independently valuable for reframing how both communities think about their shared concerns.

2. **Design Principles for OE from Improvisation:** A set of actionable design principles (e.g., "constraint-based generativity," "responsive coupling," "risk-taking protocols," "collaborative emergence architectures") for building open-ended systems.

3. **Improvisational OE Metrics:** New evaluation metrics that capture qualities valued in improvisation — responsiveness, risk, coherence, collaborative emergence — applicable to both human-AI and agent-only systems.

4. **Embodied Open-Endedness:** A theoretical and practical contribution connecting somaesthetic design to OE — showing how embodied interaction produces different (and potentially richer) OE dynamics.

5. **Agent Ethology of Improvisation:** Methods and findings from observing emergent improvisational behavior in multi-agent AI systems, connecting to the broader agent ethology research program.

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| "Just a metaphor" critique | Formal structural mapping with explicit correspondence table; computational experiments |
| Too broad scope | Focus Phase 1 paper on 2-3 key bridges; expand in subsequent papers |
| Lack of empirical grounding | Phase 3 provides empirical evidence; Phase 2 metrics enable quantitative evaluation |
| Collaborator availability | Multiple potential collaborators identified; can proceed with subset |
| Framework too abstract for practitioners | Phase 4 design study grounds the framework in concrete practice |
