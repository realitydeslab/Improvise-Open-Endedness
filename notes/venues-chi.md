# Venue Fit Analysis

## Primary Target: CHI 2027

**Conference:** ACM CHI Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems
**Dates:** May 10–14, 2027, Pittsburgh, PA, USA
**Expected Deadline:** ~September 2026 (abstract + paper, 1 week apart)

### Track Analysis

| Track | Fit | Pros | Cons |
|-------|-----|------|------|
| **Papers (main)** | ★★★★ Best fit | Highest visibility; archival; recognized contribution types include theoretical/conceptual | Competitive (~25% acceptance); requires strong empirical or analytical grounding |
| **alt.chi** | ★★★ Good fit | Welcomes boundary-pushing, interdisciplinary work; lower page count | Less prestigious; may signal "not ready for main track" |
| **Late-Breaking Work** | ★★ Fallback | Good for preliminary results; lower bar | Not archival; short format; would duplicate the WIP |
| **Journals (TOCHI/PACM)** | ★★★★ Strong alt | Rolling deadline; longer format for theoretical depth; presented at CHI | Longer review cycle; may miss CHI 2027 presentation slot |

**Recommendation:** Submit to **Papers (main track)** as primary. If rejected, revise for **TOCHI** (rolling, presented at next CHI).

### CHI 2027 Fit Assessment

**Strong fit because:**
- CHI values interdisciplinary work bridging theory and design
- Improvisation connects to active CHI research areas (co-creative AI, embodied interaction, soma design)
- Author has CHI 2026 paper (GravField) — established CHI contributor
- Design implications are central to the contribution
- Qualitative interview methodology is well-established at CHI

**Risks:**
- Reviewers unfamiliar with OE/ALife may find it too theoretical
- Framework papers without system or user study can feel undergrounded
- Need strong CHI-specific literature engagement
