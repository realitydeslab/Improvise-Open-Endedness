# Venue Scouting: Improvisation and Open-Endedness

## Recommended Venues (Ranked)

### 1. ALIFE — International Conference on Artificial Life ★★★
- **Deadline:** Typically Feb–March (papers), varies by year
- **Next:** ALIFE 2027 (TBD)
- **Why it fits:** The home of open-endedness research. OEE is a core topic. The community is actively seeking theoretical innovations and connections to creativity (see Soros et al., 2024 at ALIFE 2024). Improvisation as a lens for OE would be highly novel and relevant.
- **Format:** Full papers, workshops, art track
- **Note:** Workshop proposals due earlier — could propose "Improvisation and Open-Endedness" workshop

### 2. Artificial Life (MIT Press Journal) ★★★
- **Deadline:** Rolling submissions
- **Why it fits:** Premier journal for OE research. Recent special issues on OEE (2024). A theoretical paper bridging improvisation and OE would fit perfectly alongside the existing OE literature.
- **Impact:** High in the ALife community; indexed, DOI-assigned

### 3. CHI — ACM Conference on Human Factors in Computing Systems ★★★
- **Deadline:** Typically September
- **Next:** CHI 2027
- **Why it fits:** The premier HCI venue. Strong traditions in: co-creative AI (LuminAI papers published here), embodied interaction, somaesthetic design, more-than-human design. The human-AI improvisation angle fits perfectly. Amber already has CHI publications.
- **Format:** Full papers, alt.chi, workshops, interactivity

### 4. C&C — ACM Creativity and Cognition ★★★
- **Deadline:** Typically February–March
- **Next:** C&C 2027
- **Why it fits:** Dedicated to creativity research at the intersection of computing, arts, and design. Recent LuminAI papers published here. Would welcome the theoretical framework connecting improvisation, OE, and co-creativity.
- **Format:** Full papers, pictorials, demos

### 5. ICCC — International Conference on Computational Creativity ★★★
- **Deadline:** Typically March–April
- **Next:** ICCC 2027
- **Why it fits:** The CC community is actively engaging with OE (Soros et al., 2024). A paper positioning improvisation at the CC–OE intersection would address the community's current interests directly.
- **Format:** Full papers, demos, show-and-tell

### 6. NIME — New Interfaces for Musical Expression ★★
- **Deadline:** Typically January–February
- **Next:** NIME 2027
- **Why it fits:** Premier venue for music technology research. Strong improvisation tradition. Papers on human-AI musical improvisation, live coding, and swarm music have appeared here (OMax, Eigenfeldt).
- **Format:** Papers, performances, installations

### 7. TEI — ACM Conference on Tangible, Embedded, and Embodied Interaction ★★
- **Deadline:** Typically August
- **Next:** TEI 2027
- **Why it fits:** Focus on embodied interaction. The embodied improvisation angle — especially somaesthetic and contact improvisation with technology — would fit well. Workshops on sensory design.
- **Format:** Papers, pictorials, studios, art track

### 8. DIS — ACM Designing Interactive Systems ★★
- **Deadline:** Typically January
- **Next:** DIS 2027
- **Why it fits:** Design research venue with strong traditions in speculative design, participatory design, and more-than-human design. The design-improvisation angle (Schön, Suchman, Ingold) fits naturally.
- **Format:** Papers, pictorials, provocations

### 9. Leonardo (MIT Press Journal) ★★
- **Deadline:** Rolling submissions
- **Why it fits:** Journal of art, science, and technology. Lewis's Voyager paper was published here. Would welcome the art-science bridging nature of this work.
- **Impact:** Long history; well-read across art-tech communities

### 10. Organised Sound (Cambridge University Press) ★★
- **Deadline:** Rolling submissions, themed issues
- **Why it fits:** Journal focused on technology in music. Live coding, algorithmic composition, and interactive music systems are core topics. The musical open-endedness angle fits perfectly.
- **Format:** Journal articles, themed special issues

### 11. ACM TOCHI — Transactions on Computer-Human Interaction ★★
- **Deadline:** Rolling submissions
- **Why it fits:** Premier HCI journal. COFI paper was published here. A longer theoretical paper developing the improvisation-OE framework for HCI would have lasting impact.
- **Impact:** Top journal in HCI

### 12. Design Issues (MIT Press) ★
- **Deadline:** Rolling submissions
- **Why it fits:** Design theory journal. Giaccardi & Redström's "more-than-human design" paper appeared here. The design-theoretical angle connecting improvisation, OE, and more-than-human design fits.
- **Impact:** Influential in design research community

## Strategy Recommendations

1. **Lead with ALIFE or Artificial Life journal** — this is where the OE community lives and where the contribution is most novel
2. **Follow with CHI or C&C** — for the human-AI improvisation and embodied interaction angles
3. **Workshop at ALIFE** — propose an "Improvisation and Open-Endedness" workshop to build community
4. **Consider ICCC** for the CC bridge angle
5. **Journal version** in Artificial Life or TOCHI for the comprehensive theoretical framework
